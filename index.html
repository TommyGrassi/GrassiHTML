Mission Statement

"To end hunger and advance sustainable food systems by ensuring access to healthy food while supporting New Hampshire's small farmers."

Company Background

A sustainable corporation established in New Hampshire with the objective of addressing the United Nation's Sustainable Development Goal 2: ending world hunger. Established by CEO Cameron Bennett in 2011, this organization has maintained its focus and grown immensely. The primary objectives of the organization are to advance sustainable food systems, assist regional farmers, and ensure everyone has access to nutritious meals at affordable costs. The corporation began by marketing organic produce from nearby farms in the initial stages and has since expanded its product line to include sustainable food products, eco-friendly packaging, and educational programs on sustainable agriculture. Today, we offer a variety of services that seek to advance the work being done to solve this global issue within the state of New Hampshire. In New Hampshire, many low-income families struggle to pay for wholesome meals. At NH Meals, we strive to fix this problem and assist our neighboring communities.

Why Choose Us?

There are several reasons why clients should choose to do business with us:

  • Experience: We have the knowledge and experience to deliver top-notch goods and services thanks to our years of experience in sustainable food systems.
  • Wide Range of Products: Our business provides a wide variety of sustainably produced food items to meet various dietary requirements and preferences.
  • Support for Local Farmers: Clients help support regional farmers and advance a more robust and sustainable local food economy by choosing us.
  • Education and Awareness: We do more than just market goods. We offer educational initiatives and materials to spread awareness of zero hunger and sustainable agriculture.
  • Commitment to Quality: Our employees value our products' quality, making sure that it includes environmental friendliness, ethical sourcing, and nutrient content.
